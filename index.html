02 — Professional Summary

Full Stack Web Developer

Full stack development, to me, means owning a product across the whole stack — from the interface a user touches to the systems it runs on — and understanding both sides well enough to make good tradeoffs, not just plug pieces together.

  • End-to-end ownership — building interfaces, logic, and the tooling that ties them together, from first prototype to a deployed, working product.
  • Systems-level grounding — five years as a hardware technician and two years as a network administrator gave me a working understanding of the infrastructure code actually runs on.
  • Modern web fundamentals — HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and TypeScript as the core toolkit, applied to both greenfield builds and existing systems.
  • AI-augmented workflow — using prompt engineering and AI coding assistants to move faster, without skipping review, testing, or version control discipline.
  • Real-world delivery — shipped self-assessment tools already in use (Tobacco Free Hospital Assessment, Healthy City Assessment), not just personal experiments.
Core value proposition Able to take a project from idea to a working, deployed product without handing pieces off to other specialists — useful for teams and clients who need one person who can own the whole stack.
